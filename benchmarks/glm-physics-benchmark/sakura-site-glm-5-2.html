The Ephemeral Bloom

In the quiet tradition of sumi-e, the cherry blossom is not merely a flower — it is a moment suspended between presence and absence. Each petal carries the weight of impermanence, the beauty of mono no aware, the gentle sorrow of things that pass.

For three centuries, our garden has preserved this philosophy. The ink flows from charcoal ground on stone, the brush traces branches that reach toward nothing and everything at once. Here, you do not observe the sakura. You become part of its falling.
