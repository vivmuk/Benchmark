Executive Summary

Six models were evaluated across four pairwise benchmarks. Every model was tested with the same prompts, same token limits, and same scoring criteria. The goal: pick the best primary and fallback models for OpenClaw agent work.

Final routing recommendation:
  • Primary: Kimi K2.7-Code — fastest across the board, cheap enough, code-specialized.
  • Budget fallback: MiMo-V2.5 — 1M context, 4–5× cheaper, good when speed is less critical.
  • Long-context fallback: Qwen 3.7 Max — 1M context and strong reasoning, slower than Kimi.
  • Premium Claude option: Sonnet 5 — excellent quality, but ~5–6× more expensive than Kimi.
  • Avoid: Fable 5 for routine work; use only when 128K output is genuinely needed.

Quality Notes

  • Kimi K2.7-Code: Consistently produced structured, correct answers. Best at code generation and instruction following.
  • MiMo-V2.5: Good value, occasionally verbose. File-count answers were sometimes inconsistent (7 vs 10 vs 13).
  • Qwen 3.7 Max: Reasoning was solid but responses were slower and sometimes included visible "thinking" preamble.
  • Claude Sonnet 5: Clean, concise, accurate. Highest-quality output among all models tested.
  • Claude Fable 5: Reasoning-heavy outputs were good, but it produced a near-empty response on the ambiguous sustained-attention task.
