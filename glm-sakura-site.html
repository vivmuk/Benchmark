The Way of Blossoms

In the quiet discipline of sumi-e, the brush seeks not to imitate nature, but to capture its breath. A single stroke carries the weight of a thousand springs — the moment a petal releases its hold and surrenders to the wind.

SAKURA is an homage to mono no aware — the gentle sorrow of impermanence. Each blossom is born to fall, and in falling, reveals the profound beauty of things that do not last.

Here, ink and pigment meet handmade washi. Tradition is not preserved under glass, but practiced with hands that tremble and breathe.
